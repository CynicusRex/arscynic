Gamophobia, the fear of commitment.

A cardinal mistake in life is facing genuine love and not recognizing it. Gamophobia—the fear of commitment, philophobia—the fear of love, a dash of Madonna-whore complex, and simple inexperience. Whatever the ratio, it's a recipe for stress and heartbreak.

Claustrophobia by caressing.

I have a natural talent for running. Fast. Unfortunately it's a condition I cannot seem to contain to bipedal movement. I've run from love twice. Not because there were compatibility problems—on the contrary—but because of feeling uncomfortable, constricted. A sensation of remarkable uneasiness. Like claustrophobia, an intense urge to get out. Even a simple caress of the head could trigger it. I don't know why.

I don't even know where to go with this essay. To a psychologist would be the smart move. Before last year I've never even considered it because I assumed all of the above just means it isn't meant to be. Nonsense in hindsight. The tragedy is that sometimes it is meant to be, and leaving makes life worse for both. The converse being equally possible of course. Anyway, I've had plenty of encounters with women, and most of the time you both just feel there's no synergy. But once in a while you just meet someone and you can effortlessly keep talking. No friction, no pretending, no holding back, you're in tune with each other.

Polyamory versus monogamy.

I've never settled on whether monogamy makes sense or not. On one hand...

“It doesn't make sense!”

An affectionate punch.

What is love?

—Angelino Desmet; d M y.
