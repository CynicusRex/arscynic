Cleaning is tough.

Household cleaning is the toughest job I have ever done. It should not exist.

Preamble.

During times when I forget free will is an illusion and we are stardust floating trough space I often regret my poor life decisions. However, the advantage of an eclectic history is that it widens one's perspective and increases empathy.

Because we work too much and play too little I sought only for part-time jobs. After what turned out to be a trickier search than expected I eventually landed a household cleaning function for the CAW, a Belgian General Welfare Centre. It was the middle of winter. The news seemed like a heart-warming present. It wasn't. To those who played Max Payne, promptly imagine a blizzard breeze reverberating its soundtrack. To those who didn't, imagine roaming the snowy New York streets captured below while having hit rock-bottom.

Day two. One hour before the alarm rang I consciously tried to enjoy every bit of warmth before having trudge the snow again. Fast forward, at the end of the week I quit. The endless monotony gives one's mind free range to spiral. But the decisive factor was physical health. Even at part-time capacity of daily four hours my knees were hurting after three days. That says a lot considering I am used to pain from skateboarding where getting physically hurt by impact is routine. Either you hit the floor, or the board hits you. Therefore, allow me to compare the two and conclude that the kind of pain by repetitive strain during cleaning hits like an injury. It doesn't make me feel alive, it doesn't strengthen the body. Moreover, unlike skateboarding, one cannot simply stop working to recover as one sees fit. This meant a resounding ‚ÄúNope!‚Äù Sports has always been a crucial method of dealing with and finding a way out of adversity. Without it I wither.

But I was lucky. I could get out because for better or worse I had my parents as a safety net. Many, probably most people do not. There's a mortgage to pay and family to tend do. They have to rinse, they have to repeat. The majority of my acquaintances' family members who clean as a profession have physical health problems. Those that I know do too, and one of them wakes up at 4AM to take the train at 5AM, no fancy working from home. It doesn't take a genius to realise why being on the dole is often preferable. One friend's mother straight up quit without any possibility of severance. It's that bad.

Redundant.
