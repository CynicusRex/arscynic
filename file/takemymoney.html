What if I paid for all my free apps?

I go out of my way to find the best free software that doesn't rely on advertising. However, I've always felt kind of guilty for not showing appreciation towards these rare but excellent developers. How much would I lose anyway? Let's find out.

Requirements for consideration.

  1. No ads/trackers, whatsoever.
  2. Frequent usage or indispensable.
  3. Free. All of the features must be available for free, or at least its main features.
  4. No association with or reference to crypto“currencies” because these are greed incentivizing pyramid schemes.
  5. No already rich people/companies. Though this is difficult to verify.

Computer software.

uBlock Origin is arguably one of the most indispensable pieces of code on the World Wide Web. What's baffling is that he downright refuses any donations.

Lichess is another paragon on that front, refusing ads and trackers despite being the best chess website on the Web. I won't include lichess in the calculation because my lifetime patron account was an outlier €250 donation made in 2019, supplemented with €85.06 for clothing in 2022. This is not a brag about money, but an emphasis on how good the site is.
